Our Story

About Our Ceramic Business

Owo Ceramics Studio is a Buenos Aires based design studio founded in 2018. Our raison d'être is to help people slow down, connect with their inner self, and embrace ritual by bringing beauty, art and consciousness to their daily life.

Our studio has a deep interest in the intersection of art and craft, the handmade, and thoughtful design. We aim to create unique, functional works of art that celebrate everyday rituals and make life more fun. Each piece is made by a completely handmade process in our studio, using local resources and privileging a type of production with minimal environmental impact. We believe in revaluing manual work and crafts over industrialized processes and mass production.

In addition to creating beautiful ceramic pieces, we also host pottery classes and workshops in our Buenos Aires studio. We are constantly exploring partnerships with different organizations to help raise awareness on the issues we are passionate about and welcome collaboration with like-minded, environmentally conscious brands.

About Our Founder, Leticia Gagetti

Leticia Gagetti, the heart and soul behind Owo Ceramics, embarked on her journey with clay as a means to find stillness and connection in a fast-paced world. With a background in fine arts and a passion for sustainable living, she envisioned a space where art, utility, and mindfulness could converge.

Driven by the desire to share the therapeutic benefits of working with clay, Leticia opened Owo Ceramics Studio in 2018. Her philosophy centers around creating functional art that brings joy and presence to daily rituals, emphasizing unique, handcrafted pieces over mass production.

Leticia oversees every aspect of Owo Ceramics, from the initial design and hand-building process to glazing and firing. She also passionately leads many of the studio's popular pottery classes, sharing her expertise and fostering a supportive, creative environment for students of all skill levels.

Her work is a testament to her belief in slow living, conscious consumption, and the enduring beauty of handmade objects.
